A clear opt-in at account registration - no pre-ticked boxes
Separate consent for optional communications (newsletters, alerts)
Plain-language notices before we collect any new category of data
You may withdraw consent at any time by:
Emailing privacy@unitracko.com
Using the data settings in your UniTracko account (once available)
Withdrawing consent will not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal. It may affect your ability to use certain platform features.
1.8 Your Rights as a Data Principal
Under the DPDP Act, 2023, you have the following rights:
Right to access: Know what personal data we hold about you
Right to correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
Right to erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention obligations)
Right to grievance redressal: Raise a complaint with us - we must acknowledge within 48 -72 hours.
Right to nominate: Nominate a person to exercise your data rights on your behalf in case of incapacity
Right to complaint: Escalate unresolved grievances to the Data Protection Board of India (operational since November 2025)
To exercise any right, contact: privacy@unitracko.com with subject line 'Data Principal Request'.
1.9 Processing Data of Children
The DPDP Act defines a 'child' as a person under 18 years of age. However, the Act's consent requirement for children applies specifically where data is processed in a manner that may cause harm or involves tracking, profiling, or targeted advertising - for which parental or guardian consent is required.
For our platform:
Users under 18 must have a parent or guardian provide verifiable consent before we process their data
We do not engage in behavioural monitoring, profiling, or targeted advertising directed at minors - as prohibited under the DPDP Act
We do not collect data from children below 13 under any circumstances
Parents may contact privacy@unitracko.com to access, correct, or delete their child's data at any time
The DPDP Rules 2025 specify the mechanism for verifiable parental consent. We are implementing this as Phase 3 obligations approach the May 2027 deadline.

Support communications: Retained for 12 months

Financial/billing records: Retained for 7 years as required under applicable tax and accounting laws
Once the purpose for which data was collected is fulfilled, and no legal obligation requires retention, we will erase or anonymise the data.
1.11 Data Security

TLS 1.2+ encryption for all data in transit

Regular security audits and penetration testing
Incident response protocol in place for data breaches
In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to cause harm to you, we will notify the Data Protection Board of India and, where required, notify affected users in accordance with our obligations under the DPDP Act.
1.12 Cross-Border Data Transfers

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They serve various purposes - from keeping you logged in to helping us understand how visitors use the platform. Cookies cannot carry viruses or access other data on your device.

3.2 Types of Cookies We Use
A. Essential Cookies (Always Active)
These are required for the platform to function correctly. They cannot be switched off. Without them, features such as logging in, saving your admission plan, or maintaining session security will not work.
Session cookies — expire when you close your browser
Authentication tokens — keep you securely logged in
Security cookies — protect against CSRF and session hijacking
Load-balancing cookies — ensure platform stability
B. Analytics & Performance Cookies
These help us understand how users interact with UniTracko so we can improve the experience. All data is anonymised before analysis — we cannot identify individual users from analytics data.
Google Analytics — page views, session duration, user flow, drop-off points
Performance monitoring — page load speed, error tracking
C. Preference Cookies
These remember your choices and settings to provide a more personalised experience.
Language or regional preferences
Saved search filters or dashboard configurations

Building advertising profiles
Selling cookie-derived data to advertisers or data brokers

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:
View and delete cookies currently stored on your device
Block all cookies or only third-party cookies
Set preferences for future cookie acceptance
Please note: disabling essential cookies may significantly impact platform functionality. Disabling analytics cookies will not affect your ability to use the platform.
For guidance on managing cookies in your browser, visit the help documentation for Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge respectively.
